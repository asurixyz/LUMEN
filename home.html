Asia's Rise: A New Global Center

Asia is at the heart of the 21st century's changing global landscape. Its rapid economic growth, shifting demographics, and complex regional relationships are creating a new world order. This dashboard provides a comprehensive look at the data driving these shifts, focusing on key players: China, Japan, India, South Korea, and the ASEAN bloc.

Explore economic indicators and demographic trends to understand the rise of Asian economies and the geopolitical dynamics that define their interactions.

Global Supply Chains and Maritime Routes

Beyond national economies, this analysis delves into the vital maritime regions that power global trade. These waterways are crucial economic corridors and, in some cases, points of strategic competition. Our analysis includes data from four key maritime regions:

  • South China Sea: A critical chokepoint for global energy and a hub for Chinese investment, central to regional power rivalries.
  • Bay of Bengal: An emerging trade route that highlights global efforts to diversify supply chains.
  • East African Coast: A strategic link for trade between Asia and Africa, tracking new partnerships and investment flows.
  • Persian Gulf: The heart of global energy, where data on trade and investment reflects key players' energy security priorities.
